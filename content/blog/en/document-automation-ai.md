# Document automation with AI: less data entry, more control

In many industrial companies, a large part of the work does not happen on the line, but around documents: forms, reports, certificates, invoices, orders, evidence, checklists, and emails.

Document automation with AI reduces manual entry, errors, and review times.

## What can be automated

Common cases include:

- Extracting data from PDFs.
- Reading invoices or certificates.
- Classifying documents.
- Validating against rules.
- Summarizing reports.
- Creating tickets or tasks.

## OCR is not enough

OCR converts an image into text, but real automation requires understanding structure and context.

A complete solution may include:

- OCR.
- Business validations.
- LLM interpretation.
- Human review for exceptions.
- ERP, CRM, or document management integration.

## Where value appears

Value appears when repetitive work is reduced and traceability improves.

Useful metrics:

- Hours saved.
- Data entry errors reduced.
- Document cycle time.
- Percentage of documents processed without intervention.
- Exceptions detected on time.

## Conclusion

Document automation is not only administrative efficiency. It is operational control. It helps information move faster and with fewer errors to the teams making decisions.
