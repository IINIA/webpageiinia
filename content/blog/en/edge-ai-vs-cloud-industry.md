# Edge AI vs cloud: what works best for industrial AI

In industrial AI, architecture matters as much as the model. Choosing between edge, cloud, or hybrid affects latency, security, cost, availability, and maintenance.

## When Edge AI makes sense

Edge AI means running the model close to the data source: camera, sensor, line, or cell.

It makes sense when:

- The decision must happen in milliseconds.
- The line cannot depend on internet connectivity.
- Data is sensitive.
- Integration with PLC, HMI, or local systems is required.
- Image or video volume is high.

## When cloud makes sense

Cloud helps when elasticity, collaboration, heavy training, or historical analysis is needed.

It is useful for:

- Model training.
- Corporate dashboards.
- Multi-plant consolidation.
- Backups and central administration.
- Models that do not operate in real time.

## Hybrid architecture

Many mature industrial solutions combine both approaches.

Example:

- Edge inference for in-line inspection.
- Cloud synchronization for metrics and retraining.
- Central version management.
- Local alerts and global reports.

## Decision criteria

Evaluate:

- Required latency.
- Data sensitivity.
- Connectivity cost.
- Network availability.
- Support requirements.
- Multi-line scalability.

## Conclusion

There is no universal answer. In industry, the best architecture is the one that protects operations, keeps traceability, and scales without creating unnecessary dependency.
