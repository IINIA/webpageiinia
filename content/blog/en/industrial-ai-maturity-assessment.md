# How to assess plant maturity before implementing AI

Implementing AI without assessing maturity can lead to pilots that never scale. The technology may be correct, but if data, processes, and owners are not ready, the result remains a demo.

## Key dimensions

Before starting, review five dimensions:

1. Available data.
2. Technology infrastructure.
3. Standardized processes.
4. Internal talent.
5. Business metrics.

## Available data

AI needs examples, signals, or documents. A perfect database is not always required, but clarity about where the information lives is essential.

Useful questions:

- Is the data digitized?
- Is there enough history?
- Are defects or events labeled?
- Are standards clearly defined?

## Processes and owners

A model does not create value if nobody acts on its output. Every use case needs an operational owner.

For example, a maintenance alert must connect with a technician, supervisor, spare part, or ticket. A quality detection must connect with hold, reject, rework, or release.

## Infrastructure

Some cases need GPU, industrial cameras, edge devices, or integration with existing systems.

The question is not only whether the company has infrastructure, but whether that infrastructure supports continuous operation.

## Conclusion

Assessing maturity does not delay AI. It accelerates it. It helps select realistic use cases, correct budgets, and metrics that matter on the shop floor.
