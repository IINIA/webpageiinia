# Industrial safety with vision AI: from observation to prevention

Industrial safety depends on procedures, culture, and supervision. Computer vision can reinforce those elements by detecting risk conditions in real time.

The goal is not to surveil people. The goal is to prevent incidents and generate useful operational evidence.

## Common use cases

Vision AI can support:

- PPE detection.
- Restricted area entry detection.
- LOTO validation.
- Safe distance between people and machines.
- Presence in critical areas.
- Detection of blocked paths or abnormal conditions.

## Responsible design

A safety AI system must be designed clearly:

- What is detected.
- What action is triggered.
- Who receives the alert.
- How data misuse is prevented.
- How long evidence is stored.

## Integration with operations

Alerts must connect with existing processes. An isolated notification does not change behavior.

Better integration includes:

- Visual alerts in the area.
- EHS records.
- Audit evidence.
- Metrics by zone or shift.
- Retraining with false positives.

## Conclusion

Computer vision can move safety from after-the-fact audits to active prevention. The goal is to reduce risk without slowing down operations.
